The Headline

A one-paragraph diagnosis grounded in the data.

The Auto campaign hit clear peak performance in May–June 2025, generating ~200 leads/month, 8–9 sold policies, and a $3.72 CPL at a $88 policy CPA — well below the auto benchmark range of $150–400. After June 2025, average bids were pulled down from $3.09 → $2.06–$2.30, win rate collapsed from 3.18% → 0.18%, and ad-wall position drifted from ~4th to 7th. Three of the most productive sources (Premium Referral 2, Premium Paid Search, Premium Paid Search 7) either went inactive or stopped getting opportunities. This is a classic bid & allocation problem, not a market or audience problem. The recovery plan is to restore the May/June 2025 conditions: a modest base-bid raise, reactivation of three dormant winners, and concentrated multiplier increases on 6 sources priced to just above 2nd-position market RPC.

What Drove the Peak

The five factors that made May/June 2025 work — and the recipe to recreate it.

1. Higher, Confident Bidding

Average bid sat at $2.76–$3.09 (vs $2.30 today). That extra ~25¢ pushed Supreme Choice into ad-wall positions 3–4, where 50%+ of click volume lives. Win rate ran 3.18% — 17× today's level.

2. Premium Referral Engine

Premium Referral and Premium Referral 2 together delivered 22 of 29 sold policies over the past 12 months. Premium Referral 2 alone produced 36 quotes from $632 in spend — a $17 cost per quote.

3. Email Channel Live

Email contributed 120 leads, 8 quotes, 2 sales on $595 in spend. Currently the multiplier is throttled to 51% — starving 5,147 monthly opportunities of competitive bids.

4. Premium Paid Search 2 Active

15 quotes from $477 spend. Still active but bid multiplier was cut to 69%, meaning effective bid ($2.06) is half the market 2nd-position RPC of $5.05.

5. Tight, Non-Standard Targeting

Filters held to 1-driver/1-vehicle households (96% of all policies came from this segment). Currently Insured = No filter remained intact — preserving the non-standard ICP fit (DUI, accidents, lapsed coverage prospects).

6. Healthy Spend Pace

Monthly spend of ~$750 was sustained for two consecutive months without CPA breakdown. Today's $36/month pace is too thin to win meaningful auctions across the 200+ source network.

What Drifted Away

Each of these is fixable in the dashboard today.

Bids Pulled Down

Avg bid drifted from $3.09 (May '25) → $2.30 (May '26). A 21% bid cut while CA market RPCs held steady. Win rate fell 94% as a direct result.

Premium Paid Search Deactivated

Currently flagged INACTIVE. Last year delivered 7 quotes and 2 sold policies from $277 in spend. No reason in the data for this source to sit dormant.

Premium Paid Search 7 Deactivated

Currently INACTIVE. Last year: 4 quotes, 1 sold policy, $203 spend. Multiplier sits at 301% — ready to fire if reactivated.

Premium Referral 2 Has Zero Opportunities

Marked Active, but 0 opportunities in the last 60 days despite producing 8 sold policies and 36 quotes last year. Indicates a routing issue or upstream targeting/eligibility filter to investigate with the GOAL product team.

Premium Referral Multiplier Cut

Was the #1 source last year (14 sales, $1,200 spend). Multiplier now sits at 86% — effective bid $2.47 vs market 2nd-position RPC of $5.87. Bidding 58% below market on the top revenue source.

Email Throttled

Multiplier at 51% on a source with 5,147 monthly opportunities and proven sale conversion. Effective bid $1.55 vs market 1st-position RPC of $4.79.

Recovery Action Plan — 7 Moves

Calibrated against the California Auto RightPricing market data. Target: just above 2nd-position RPC plus a 10% volatility buffer.

1. Raise Base Bid: $2.86 → $3.00

High Priority

Restores the May 2025 peak bid level. Single dial that resets the entire campaign closer to market without having to re-tune every source. Expected to lift win rate from 0.18% to the 1.0–2.0% range immediately while we layer in source-level moves.

Why now: Avg bid is the strongest single predictor of position and win rate in the trend data. The peak month had avg bid $3.09; today is $2.30.

2. Reactivate Premium Paid Search and Premium Paid Search 7

High Priority

Both currently Inactive. Combined last-year contribution: 11 quotes, 3 sold policies on $480 spend. No data justifies keeping them off. Set Premium Paid Search to 180% multiplier (target market 2nd-position $5.05 RPC at $3.00 base). Keep Premium Paid Search 7 at its existing 301% multiplier on activation — 2nd-position RPC is $6.73 and 301% of $3.00 = $9.03 (10% buffer above target).

Why now: Reactivation is a one-click change. Historical data shows immediate productivity, no ramp.

3. Boost Premium Referral: 86% → 200%

High Priority

Top revenue source last year (14 policies, $1,200 spend, $86 CPA). At 86% multiplier the effective bid is $2.47 — well below the market 2nd-position RPC of $5.87. Move multiplier to 200% (effective bid $6.00) to land just above 2nd position. Expect this single change to add 4–6 sold policies/month at peak pace.

Why now: This is the highest-leverage move in the campaign. Largest historical revenue contribution + largest underbid gap.

4. Investigate Premium Referral 2 Opportunity Drought

Medium Priority — Investigation

Source is marked Active with a 90% multiplier, but it's received 0 opportunities in 60 days despite 36 quotes and 8 policies last year. This isn't a bid problem — it's a routing or eligibility problem. Open a ticket with Chris/GOAL product team to confirm the source is properly mapped to Supreme Choice's California non-standard targeting. Once opportunities flow, raise multiplier to 200% (matches Premium Referral target).

Why now: This was the #2 source for sold policies last year. A routing fix could unlock 6+ quotes/month with no additional cost beyond bid spend.

5. Boost Email: 51% → 195%

Medium Priority

5,147 monthly opportunities are sitting under-bid. Effective bid jumps from $1.55 to $5.85 — just above 2nd-position RPC of $5.78. Expect this to start producing 30–50 leads/month with a contact and quote rate consistent with last year's 6.7% lead-to-quote rate.

Why now: Highest opportunity volume of the proven-converter sources. Raising the multiplier here is the cheapest way to recover lead volume.

6. Boost Premium Paid Search 2: 69% → 180%

Medium Priority

15 quotes and 2 policies last year. Effective bid moves from $2.06 to $5.40, putting us just above 2nd-position RPC of $5.05. Expect ~10 leads/month at this bid level, consistent with last year's average.

Why now: A concentrated bet on a known winner — small absolute spend increase, predictable lead volume return.

7. Hold Steady — Sources Already Aligned

Monitor Only

Five sources are already priced sensibly relative to market and should not be changed: Premium Paid Search 4 (301%, max'd out), Premium Paid Search 6 (164% — aligned with $4.53 RPC), Premium Paid Search 2B (197% — aligned with $4.99 RPC), Premium Email B (249% — aligned with $6.92 RPC), Internal SEO (197% — aligned with $5.67 RPC). Watch for changes after the base-bid raise; trim if any begin to outpace CPA targets.

Why now: These are not the bottleneck. Changing them would dilute focus and risk triggering changes that aren't necessary.

Targeting Stays Lean

A reminder of the non-standard ICP — and why we're not changing filters.

Supreme Choice serves a non-standard auto market that competing agencies typically avoid: consumers with prior accidents, DUIs, lapsed coverage, or who currently have no insurance. The campaign's "Currently Insured = No" filter is intentional and stays in place. We do not target standard insured comparison shoppers — that's not the book Supreme Choice is trying to grow.

The bid moves above are aimed exclusively at winning more of the right kind of leads. By concentrating budget on the 6 sources that historically produced policies for this audience, we keep the funnel tight and the lead-to-sale CVR strong (~3% at peak — well above the 2% benchmark).
