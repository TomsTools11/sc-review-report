09

ICP — non-standard, unchanged

Supreme Choice serves a non-standard auto market that competing agencies typically avoid: consumers with prior accidents, DUIs, lapsed coverage, or who currently have no insurance. The campaign's "Currently Insured = No" filter is intentional and stays in place. The v2 bid moves above are aimed exclusively at winning more of the right kind of leads.

By concentrating budget on the 6 sources that historically produced policies for this audience — and now adding Internal SEO 2 as a seventh — we keep the funnel tight and the lead-to-sale CVR strong (~3% at peak, well above the 2% benchmark). The SR-22 / DUI positive-modifier test is still queued for after Premium Referral 2 routing is fixed and Auto volume returns.

Methodology & data sources. RightPricing data: trailing 7d CA Auto pull dated 5/22/2026. Position breakdowns (1st / 2nd / 3rd / 4+) used to anchor target effective bids. 30-day source data: GOAL Source-Settings + Analytics exports dated 5/22/2026. Base bid: $3.00, confirmed against realized max bids on cap-multiplier sources (PPS 4 at 301% → $9.03 max; PPS 7 at 301% → $9.03 max). Effective bid = base bid × (multiplier / 100). Target rule: for sources where the market 2nd-position RPC is reachable within platform caps, target eff bid = 2nd RPC + 5–10% buffer; where 2nd is unreachable, hold at platform cap or pull to a sensible position level. Right-pricing philosophy: don't price every source to market — concentrate budget on the 4–8 historical winners and cap the over-aggressive bids on costly long-tail sources. All conversion metrics calculated based on Leads, not Clicks.
