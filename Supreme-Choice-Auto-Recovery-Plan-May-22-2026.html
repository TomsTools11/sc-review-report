What's new in v2

The market shifted, the plan got smarter, and three sources need to come down. Pulling fresh RightPricing CA Auto data on 5/22 surfaced three things the 5/8 plan didn't have visibility to: (1) the 2nd-position market RPC for Email dropped from $5.78 to $4.37, which means the 165% multiplier we set is already correct — the old plan's "push to 195%" is stale and gets dropped; (2) Internal SEO 2 is a latent winner with 321 weekly clicks and a $5.64 2nd-position RPC, currently bid at just 114% — new push to 210%; (3) three sources are wildly over-bidding the market and burning budget for nothing (Paid Search at 195% mult vs market 2nd of $2.53, etc.) — pulling them frees roughly $10-15/mo at current volume. We're also dropping the Premium Paid Search reactivation from the plan — the base PPS source can't be reactivated, only the numbered variants. The single biggest unblocker is still Premium Referral 2 routing — 0 opportunities in 60+ days on a source that produced 8 policies last year.

ICP — non-standard, unchanged

Supreme Choice serves a non-standard auto market that competing agencies typically avoid: consumers with prior accidents, DUIs, lapsed coverage, or who currently have no insurance. The campaign's "Currently Insured = No" filter is intentional and stays in place. The v2 bid moves above are aimed exclusively at winning more of the right kind of leads.

By concentrating budget on the 6 sources that historically produced policies for this audience — and now adding Internal SEO 2 as a seventh — we keep the funnel tight and the lead-to-sale CVR strong (~3% at peak, well above the 2% benchmark). The SR-22 / DUI positive-modifier test is still queued for after Premium Referral 2 routing is fixed and Auto volume returns.

Methodology & data sources

RightPricing data: trailing 7d CA Auto pull dated 5/22/2026. Position breakdowns (1st / 2nd / 3rd / 4+) used to anchor target effective bids. 30-day source data: GOAL Source-Settings + Analytics exports dated 5/22/2026. Base bid: $3.00 confirmed against realized max bids on cap-multiplier sources (PPS 4 at 301% → $9.03 max; PPS 7 at 301% → $9.03 max). Effective bid = base bid × (multiplier / 100). Target rule: for sources where the market 2nd-position RPC is reachable within platform caps, target eff bid = 2nd RPC + 5-10% buffer. For sources where 2nd is unreachable, hold at platform cap or pull to a sensible position level. Right-pricing philosophy per Tom's account-management rule: don't price every source to market — concentrate budget on the 4-8 historical winners and cap the over-aggressive bids on costly long-tail sources.
