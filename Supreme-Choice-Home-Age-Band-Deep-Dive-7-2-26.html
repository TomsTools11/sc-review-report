Investigations

Open with GOAL · July 2, 2026

  1. 1
    Ad-group age settings. Ages under 55 are 89.0% of all-time searches and 21.7% of the last 30 days. Verify the ad group’s age range and any age modifiers with GOAL before new age changes ship.
  2. 2
    Export reconciliation, ages 66+. The all-time export shows 40–66 searches per single age at 66+; the last-30-day export shows 180–280 per single age. A same-scope all-time pull must contain the last 30 days, so one export is incomplete. Re-pull both with GOAL support before either is treated as the source of truth.
  3. 3
    Age-45 bucket. Age 45 shows 31,525 searches at a 0.18% win rate; neighboring single ages run 3,500–8,900 searches at 0.5–1.1%. Confirm with GOAL whether unknown consumer ages report as 45.
  4. 4
    June 19 age-chart source. The June 19 review charted 2,183 leads and 302 policies by age (65+ at 65% of leads). The campaign’s all-time export shows 1,770 leads and 55 policies (65+ at 6.7% of leads and calls). The June 19 chart reflects a different dataset — re-source it before it informs first-call priorities or bids.
Sections 01–03 use the all-time export as provided. Item 2 does not change band rankings for contacts or policies; it affects the size of the 65+ segment.

Recommendations

1
Hold the base bid at $4.15; ship the July 2 source changeset first
The pending July 2 bundle (bid-schedule normalization plus the Tier 1, Tier 2, Tier 3, and Home SearchB resets) ships before any age change, so each change reads cleanly at the day-14 checkpoint.
2
Resolve the section 05 verifications before age changes ship
Items 1 and 2 (ad-group age settings; export reconciliation) gate every step below. Items 3 and 4 (age-45 bucket; June 19 chart source) proceed in parallel.
3
Set a minimum consumer age of 18
Ages 16–17: 25 leads, 0 contacted, 0 quoted, $83.71 all-time. This is the only hard filter in the set; all other age changes are modifiers.
4
Day-14: up-weight ages 55–64 and 35–44 to 110%
55–64 contacts at 27.1% ($16.12 per contact) with 9 policies at $137.95; 35–44 binds 18 policies at $81.31. All-time average bids at 55–64 ran $2.25–$2.42 against a $2.77 campaign average. Age modifiers stack with source multipliers; steps stay at 110% pending the next market pull.
5
Day-14: down-weight ages 25–34 to 85%
2 policies on $942.19 ($471.09 per policy) at a 5.1% contact rate. A modifier, not an exclusion — the band keeps serving at reduced weight.
6
Hold 18–24, 45–54, and 65+ at 100%
18–24 carries 13 policies at $144.76 (ages 19, 20, and 22 carry all 13). 45–54 holds pending the age-45 bucket check — a down-weight before item 3 of section 05 resolves could suppress unknown-age traffic broadly. 65+ already holds 57.0% of last-30-day searches; re-check after the export reconciliation.
