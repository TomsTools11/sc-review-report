04

Tier 1 — the unexpected turnaround

The fix happened on its own — the publisher audit was unnecessary.

Tier 1 had been the single largest open item from the 4/17 review. The plan was a publisher audit through Nick and Chris to cross-reference current publisher IDs against the 2025 blacklist. On the 5/8 call, Monty was told the publisher pins are rolled regularly (like API keys), making any audit effectively impossible to action.

Tier 1 contact rate jumped 9.5% → 28.1% with no bid change and no audit. Effective bid unchanged at $3.55. Lead volume nearly unchanged (35 → 32). What changed is which publishers actually delivered — and they're suddenly answering. Quote CPA fell from $53.15 to $24.58 (below gold). 6 quotes from this source in 22 days, materially above prior pace.

Working hypothesis: the same upstream publisher rotation that made the audit impossible also rotated the bad publishers out this cycle. Hold the multiplier and watch — if Tier 1 holds 25%+ contact rate for the next 14 days, the recovery is real. If it slips below 15%, the bad publishers rotated back in and we re-escalate to Nick and Chris with this data as evidence.

Methodology. Home CA campaign only. Contact rate = Contacted ÷ Leads, calculated per source per period from GOAL platform exports.
