Tier 1 — the unexpected turnaround

Tier 1 had been the single largest open item from the 4/17 review. The plan was a publisher audit through Nick and Chris to cross-reference current publisher IDs against the 2025 blacklist. On the 5/8 call, Monty was told that the publisher pins are rolled regularly (like API keys), making any audit effectively impossible to action.

Tier 1 contact rate jumped 9.5% → 28.1% with no bid change and no audit. Effective bid unchanged at $3.55. Lead volume nearly unchanged (35 → 32). What changed is which publishers actually delivered — and they're suddenly answering. Quote CPA fell from $53.15 to $24.58 (below gold). 6 quotes from this source in 22 days, materially above prior pace.

Working hypothesis: the same upstream publisher rotation that made the audit impossible also rotated the bad publishers out of the mix this cycle. The fix happened on its own. What we do now is hold the multiplier and watch — if Tier 1 holds 25%+ contact rate for the next 14 days, the recovery is real. If it slips back below 15%, the bad publishers rotated back in and we re-escalate to Nick and Chris with this data as evidence.

Methodology

Home CA campaign only. Contact Rate = Contacted ÷ Leads, calculated per source per period from GOAL platform exports. Comparison points: May 1–22 2026 MTD (latest, 22 days), trailing 30 days through 5/13 (last review), trailing 30 days through 5/8, April 2026 (calendar month). Months with strong inbound calls can produce contact-rate values above 100% in the underlying data; those are capped at 100% on charts for readability. Quote CPA = Cost ÷ Quotes; Policy CPA = Cost ÷ Policies bound. Sources with fewer than 5 clicks in a window are excluded from per-source charts. ICP filter "Currently Insured = No" remains in place — this is the non-standard-market design and is not a defect. Volume-metric comparisons across periods of different lengths (22d vs 30d) are not directly comparable; rate metrics are.
