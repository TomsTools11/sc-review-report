03The Winning Formula

Six levers that made your campaign an outlier

ICP Lock

Currently Insured = No

Load-bearing filter set from day one. Your non-standard carrier mix (E&S, Fair Plans, SR-22 eligible) uniquely qualifies you to write this book. Nobody else on our platform has this positioning.

Ad Verbiage

"Call us for a free quote"

Single biggest conversion lever in 10 months of history. Specific call-to-action copy drove a 550% jump in inbound calls when introduced in Sep 2025. Pivot from form-first to phone-first.

Automation

Agency Zoom Sequences

Text + email automation fires within minutes of the lead hitting your CRM. Infrastructure that makes 4.5-month delayed sales (Cathy Dillon) possible and lets auto remain viable despite structural headwinds.

Off-Hours Bidding

The 90-Minute Kick

Base bid at 100%, manually kicked to 130% only if the first 90 minutes of the day are quiet. Smarter than a flat high bid — preserves efficient economics while getting volume when you need it.

Day-Parting

2:00 PM Lunch Cap

Leads shut off at 2:00 PM, resume at 3:30–4:00 PM, stop for the day after. Prevents pile-ups during no-one-home windows. This single setting preserves your contact rate.

Counter-Positioning

Keep What Others Cut

Coastal/hurricane counties, DFW hail zones, Harris & Cameron County — everyone else excludes them. You keep them in. Less auction competition, and your carrier mix can actually write the business.

04The September 2025 Playbook

What happened, what we fixed, and why it matters for today

The setup: September volume tripled from August, but sales went down by half. Same producer, same process. The problem wasn't the filter — it was who the publishers were actually sending.

Month Leads Quotes Sales Quote-to-Bind CPA
August 2025 ~40 11 4 36% ~$40
September 2025 ~120 28 2 7% ~$175
October 2025 ~153 29 8 27% $75
Late October 2025 — 23 8 34.7% $37

The five moves that recovered the campaign, in order of impact:

  1. Advertiser blacklist. Specific advertiser IDs (2409, 3954) repeatedly sent insured comparison shoppers despite a compliant filter. Pulled those IDs from Tier 1.
  2. Bid down high-volume channels. Tier 1 dropped 16% ($172→$144), Tier 3 dropped 18% ($99→$81). If a channel is sending junk, stop paying premium for it.
  3. Bid up the arbitrage channel. Home Search / QuoteFunnel pushed from $1.95 to $2.63 per click (35% jump) because its quote-to-bind was 50%.
  4. Shut off dead auto channels. Premium Page Search Inventory #5 on auto: 18 leads, 1 contact in 60 days. Cut it.
  5. Stop tweaking once it's fixed. Your rule: "If it ain't broke, don't fix it." Exactly right.

Why auto is structurally harder

  • Different producer — home agent is the strong closer, auto agent is junior. Producer skill is part of the gap.
  • Auto lead behavior is structural: people shop auto at work when they can't take calls. QuoteWizard sees the same pattern.
  • Auto lead composition is messier — currently-insured shoppers, homeowners with package deals, etc.

What's worth testing

  • Tighten the auto filter the way home is tight: +no current insurance, +accidents/DUI as positive modifiers, -homeowners as negative.
  • Test SR-22 as a positive modifier — your carrier mix can handle it.
  • Delayed sales pattern proves the automation works on auto over time (Cathy Dillon: June lead → Oct bind). Worth keeping warm.
