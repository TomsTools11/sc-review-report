Optimization Plan — What's Next
Where We See Opportunity

Your campaigns are delivering leads at a strong $6.97 blended CPL and your team is converting those leads effectively — 28.4% contact rate and 17.6% quote rate are well above average. The biggest opportunity is winning more auctions. Right now your campaigns are winning fewer than 1% of eligible auctions, which means there's significant room to grow lead volume without changing anything about your targeting or lead quality.

We've completed a detailed pricing analysis of the California Home Insurance market and identified specific adjustments to how your campaign competes in each traffic source. These changes are designed to increase your win rate from ~1% to 3–5%, which should translate to more leads per day at roughly the same quality you're seeing today.

Source-Level Bid Calibration In Progress

We've analyzed 7 days of market pricing data across all traffic sources in California Home Insurance. Several sources were bidding below the competitive threshold needed to win auctions consistently. We're adjusting each source's bid multiplier individually to match current market pricing — raising bids where you're being outcompeted, and slightly reducing where you're overpaying. This is precision tuning, not a blanket increase.

Schedule Optimization Recommended

Your campaign currently runs weekdays 9am–4pm PT. We see an opportunity to capture additional leads by extending to Saturdays (9am–2pm trial). Weekend searchers tend to be high-intent since they're actively shopping when most agencies are less responsive — giving you an edge.

Targeting Filters No Change

Your "Currently Insured = No" filter is working as intended — it ensures every lead you receive is an uninsured homeowner actively looking for coverage. This filter narrows the eligible traffic pool significantly, but the leads that get through are high-quality. We'll keep this in place.

Budget Ceiling Watch Monitor

With the daily limit raised to $40, you have room to absorb the increased win rate from bid calibration. As your campaigns start winning more auctions, you may begin hitting the daily cap more frequently. If that happens, it's a positive sign — it means the campaign is competing effectively. We'll flag if we see the cap limiting your growth so we can discuss options.
