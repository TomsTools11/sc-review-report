Quote CPA broke below the gold standard at $20.70 — and Tier 1 contact rate recovered organically. The recovery accelerated through May. Tier 2 remains the workhorse (3 of 3 policies, Quote CPA $8.23) and contact rate climbed to 35.5%. The path back to the Aug–Oct 2025 peak is no longer hypothetical — we're in it.

07

Tier 1 — the unexpected turnaround

The audit we were told was impossible appears to have happened on its own.

Tier 1 was flagged on 5/13 as the last unfinished item from the 4/17 review. The plan was a publisher audit through Nick/Chris to cross-reference current publisher IDs against the 2025 blacklist. On the 5/8 call, Monty was told that publisher pins are rolled regularly (like API keys), making the audit effectively impossible to action. We held the multiplier at 115% and committed to monitoring.

Tier 1 contact rate jumped 9.5% → 28.1% with no intervention. Effective bid unchanged at $3.55 (below 4th-position market). Lead volume nearly unchanged (35 → 32). What changed is which publishers actually delivered — and they're suddenly answering. Quote CPA fell from $53.15 to $24.58 (below gold). 6 quotes from this source in 22 days, double the prior 30-day pace.

Working hypothesis: the same upstream publisher rotation that made the audit impossible also rotated the bad publishers out this cycle. We don't have to chase the audit anymore — the data is telling us the issue resolved itself. The watch-item now is whether Tier 1 holds, or slips back into the under-10% zone with the next rotation. Treat this as watch-and-wait, not a victory lap.
