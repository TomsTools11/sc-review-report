Conversion metrics based on Leads + Calls (prospects), not Clicks.
Data pulled June 7, 2026.

Home is carrying the account; Auto is priced out of its market. Over the last 30 days (May 8 – Jun 6), CA Home delivered 169 leads + 24 calls, 23 quotes, and 2 bound policies on $674.20 (Quote CPA $29.31), while CA Auto delivered 28 leads + 2 calls, 2 quotes, and 0 policies on $274.38 — an 0.84% win rate at average position 11.3, far below the visible ad wall.

Three attribute signals stand out for targeting: Mobile Home (12 of 17 Home policies this year at a $92.90 CPA vs $773.95 for Single Family), Condominium (68.6% quote rate this year), and age 55–74 (85% of all-time Home policies; the best Auto contact rates at 26–29% and a $38.46 Auto CPA in the 65–74 band). The biggest channel unlocks: Home Tier 1 (32,872 opportunities, 0.8% win rate — bidding 2nd-position money in a market where 94% of clicks go to 1st) and Auto Native/Display/Social (28,602 opportunities, 3,642 bids, zero wins). All KPIs in this report are calculated per campaign — nothing is blended.

Where to lean in

Attributes with demonstrated higher quote/bind rates, and the channels that can be capitalized on — per campaign

CA Home — target harder

  • Mobile Home: 70.6% of this year's policies, $92.90 CPA, 15.6% quote-to-bind. Prioritize speed-to-lead on every MH lead; weight bid increases toward Tier 2/3 where MH lives.
  • Condominium: 68.6% quote rate this year on 35 prospects, 1.09% win rate — high-quality pocket we barely buy. Tier 1 fix captures it.
  • Age 55–74: 60.8% of all-time policies at $18–19 CPA. Mix already skews here; protect it.
  • Channel — Tier 1: 32,872 opps at 0.8% win. The single biggest Home volume unlock (see Right-Pricing report).
  • Channel — Tier 3 bid rate: 25.2% win when it bids, but bids on only 2.2% of 9,406 opps. Investigation item.

CA Auto — target harder

  • Age 55–74: 26–29% contact, $38–$121 CPA. First-call priority for CC; future bid-weight candidate.
  • Age 35–44: most bound policies (17) at $79.42 CPA — the volume segment worth protecting as bids rise.
  • Channel — Native/Display/Social: 28,602 opps, zero wins at an at-market bid. Smallest multiplier nudge with the largest reach (145→160).
  • Channel — Email + PPS 2 + PPS 3: 1st position clears at just $5.50–$5.99; all three are within reach under the current base bid.
  • Channel — PPS 5C: 589 market clicks/mo, currently bid at $2.29 vs $6.22 2nd-position — the most mispriced single source in the account.
  • Deprioritize (don't exclude): 25–34 ($451 CPA) and 45–54 ($356 CPA) get worked after the priority bands.

Path to John's stated goal: 8+ clients/month

April booked 4 new clients; John wants at least double. The combined plan — Home Tier 1 repricing (+6–8 quotes/mo projected), the Auto re-price (2–4 policies/mo once mature), and MH/Condo/55+ lead prioritization — projects a credible route to 8–9 combined policies/month, contingent on contact rate holding ≥17% as volume scales. Methodology & data notes

ItemDetail
Data window"Last 30 days" = approx. May 8 – Jun 6, 2026 (exports pulled Jun 7). Age & credit groupings are all-time; property type shown for last-30-day, this-year, last-year.
SourcesGOAL dashboard exports in goal-exporfts/ (campaign performance, groupings by source/age/county/credit/property type, source settings) + RightPricing reports (CA Auto, CA Home) + Fathom transcript of the 5/8/26 review with John Hunt.
DefinitionsProspects = Leads + Calls. Contact rate = Contacted / Prospects. Quote rate = Quotes / Prospects. Quote-to-bind = Policies / Quotes. Win rate and Avg Position as reported by the platform. Campaigns are never blended.
Maturity caveatLast-30-day bind counts are immature — May/June leads are still being worked, and policy dispositions lag (last import 6/3). All-time groupings include the stronger 2025 market; use them for relative ranking, not absolute forecasting.
Data artifactsA few age bands show quotes ≥ contacted (disposition timing). Auto "Tier" rows and Benepath rows in RightPricing are cross-vertical ghosts and were excluded. Credit attribute is single-segment in both campaigns.
