What this means — three findings to discuss

1
Tier 1 has gone from a contact-rate liability to a top performer.

In the prior six months Tier 1 sat at 26.7% contact rate on 165 leads — well below benchmark. In the last six months it jumped to 63.6% on 118 leads, a +36.9 point swing. Whatever the team has changed about Tier 1 lead handling is working — keep it up.

2
Tier 2 and Home SearchB are the volume + contactability double threat.

Tier 2 delivered 209 leads in the last six months at 53.1% contact rate. Home SearchB delivered 129 leads at 52.7%. These are the right places to add budget — they combine real volume with the highest contact rates we have. Lifetime cost per contact is in the $6–7 range.

3
Tier 3 is the one to keep an eye on.

Tier 3 has delivered the most lifetime leads (734) but at the lowest contact rate (32.0% lifetime). Volume has pulled back sharply in the last six months (28 leads vs. 233 prior). We should investigate whether there's a lead-quality reason it's harder to reach Tier 3 prospects, and decide whether to filter further or shift that budget to Tier 2 / Home SearchB.

Recommended next steps

  1. Lean budget into Tier 2 and Home SearchB. These are the workhorses for contact rate right now and should absorb additional spend before any other source.
  2. Codify what changed on Tier 1. The contact-rate transformation is the biggest swing in the dataset. Document the cause (lead-handling change, dialer settings, ad-group adjustments) so we can replicate it on Tier 3.
  3. Investigate Tier 3 contactability. Pull a sample of Tier 3 leads from the last 90 days, listen to dial attempts, and confirm whether the issue is lead quality, timing, or workflow. Decide on next-step targeting once we know.
  4. Hold steady on Home Search and NA. Both deliver excellent contact rates but at smaller volume. They're working — leave them alone unless we identify scale-up levers.
Methodology. Contact Rate = Contacted ÷ Leads, calculated per source from GOAL platform exports. Recent 6 months = Nov 2025 – Apr 2026. Prior 6 months = May 2025 – Oct 2025. May 2026 is excluded from period comparisons because it is a partial month. Sources with fewer than 5 lifetime leads are omitted from rankings to avoid noise. Months with strong inbound call traffic can produce contact-rate values above 100% in the underlying data; these are capped at 100% on the trend chart for readability.
