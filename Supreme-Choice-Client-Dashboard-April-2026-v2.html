Next Steps

Every action below maps to more sales or more qualified conversations with non-standard prospects
Sales Lever #1

Tighten Lead Follow-Up to Lift Contact Rate

The biggest single lever on sales right now is contact rate. Moving from 18.6% toward 30% would unlock 4 to 6 additional quotes monthly at current spend. Recommend reviewing speed-to-first-dial, SMS cadence, and multi-attempt rules on Tier 1 and Tier 2 leads first.

Sales Lever #2

Concentrate Budget on Binding Sources

Tier 1 produced both binds this month. Tier 2 is the quote volume engine. Proposal: shift a portion of Home Search spend (currently highest cost per quote, zero binds) toward Tier 1 and Tier 2 where non-standard match is proven.

Hold Steady

Keep the Currently Insured Filter

The "Currently Insured = No" filter is doing exactly what it should. Leads arriving are matched to your non-standard carriers, and the 76% quote-on-contact rate proves the pipeline quality. No change.

Monitor

Home Search Cost Trend

Home Search and Home SearchB are the highest-cost sources per quote with no binds yet. Home SearchB is trending in the right direction over the last two weeks. If improvement does not hold into May, we will recalibrate bids.

Continuing

Reinforce Non-Standard Risk Signals

SR-22, DUI, prior-claims, and lapsed-coverage indicators remain the quality markers for your book. Ongoing optimizations will keep reinforcing these signals, not broaden the net.

Tracking

Measuring What Matters

Going forward we will track two numbers weekly: bound policies and qualified conversations with non-standard prospects. Everything else is a supporting metric that serves those two outcomes.
