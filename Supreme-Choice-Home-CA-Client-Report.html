The Story Since Our Last Review

The bigger structural fix — repricing the campaign so we land in front of the highest-intent shoppers, not the residual ones — is queued up for next week. That's where Policy CPA goes from where it sits today ($310) to where you want it ($75 acceptable, $35 gold).

What's Coming Next Week

Three coordinated moves, sequenced so we isolate the variables and can read the impact cleanly.

Step 1 · This Week

Audit the Tier 1 publisher mix for drift

Our largest traffic source has 27,000+ auction opportunities this month but is winning a much smaller share than expected — even at competitive bids. The pattern matches what we saw during the 2025 lead drought, when a single drifted publisher dragged performance down across the source. We'll cross-reference the publisher IDs against the bad-list pattern from peak era and clean the mix before pushing more spend through.

Step 2 · Monday 5/4

Re-anchor bids to current market and concentrate budget on proven winners

We'll re-base the campaign to $4.15 (the current market rate for above-2nd-position on our anchor source) and lift the multipliers on the 4 sources that are converting (Tier 1, Tier 2, Home Search, Home SearchB). Total spend stays roughly flat — we're reallocating, not adding.

Special play on Home Search: we'll target the 3rd-position market price (~$11/click), not the 1st or 2nd. This mirrors the August/September 2025 pattern where you were paying around $2/click in a $10–12 market and capturing residual high-intent traffic. Same idea, applied to today's pricing.

Step 3 · Monday 5/4

Normalize the daily-schedule pricing and free up the manual kick

We'll flatten the day-parting modifiers to 100% and let the source-level pricing do all the bidding work. The off-hours manual 130% kick stays available for slower mornings — exactly the lever you described on 4/6. This makes the campaign more predictable and easier to read week-to-week.

14-day checkpoint: We'll re-pull the market pricing report and source-level data on 5/13. If Quote CPA holds in the $30–50 range and Policy CPA compresses toward $100, the changes are working as expected. If anything trips a stop-loss (Q-CPA > $100 on Home Search, for example), we adjust immediately.
