Data pulled June 7, 2026.

Diagnosis: a bidding problem, not a demand or quality problem. Demand is intact — 5,184 searches entered auctions in the last 30 days, and 28,602 opportunities sit on Native/Display/Social alone. But the campaign won 0.84% of auctions at an average position of 11.3, producing 30 prospects, 2 quotes, and 0 policies on $274.38. At the May–Jun 2025 peak, the same campaign won 3.1–3.3% at position ~3.8–4.1 and produced 200+ leads and 8–9 policies a month on ~$750.

What changed is price: market RPCs inflated (1st position now clears $5.50–$5.99 on the workhorse paid-search sources, $8.61 on Native, up to $34 on premium variants) while the base bid stayed locked at $2.86. The May 13 multiplier round helped, but multipliers alone have hit their limit — 8 of 24 active sources mathematically cannot reach their target market band even at the 301% ceiling (a ninth gets there only by maxing out). The plan: a two-wave multiplier re-price now (within the locked base), a base-bid decision at this review, and age-prioritized lead handling to lift contact rate.

02

Three-gate diagnosis

Target Rate → Bid Rate → Win Rate, evaluated in order

Gate 1 · Target rate

Low, by design

Target rates run 0.2–40% across sources. This reflects the intentional non-standard ICP filtering (Currently-Insured stays as configured — confirmed program design, not a defect). No filter changes recommended.

Accepted — structural
Gate 2 · Bid rate

Low, partly explainable

Bid rates run 3.7–25.9%. Single ad group, so cannibalization is unlikely — and sub-floor effective bids likely suppress auction entry on several sources. Two true anomalies: PPS 6 (90 opps) and Native 2 (52 opps) placed zero bids. Verify with Chris; expect bid rates to lift as bids clear market floors.

Investigate 2 anomalies
Gate 3 · Win rate

The failing gate

Where bids ARE placed, they lose: Native 0.00% on 3,642 bids, Email 1.73%, PPS 3 0.78%, Internal SEO winning impressions at position 8.6 but zero clicks. This is the one scenario where raising bids is the correct lever — calibrated against RightPricing below.

Action — re-price

3 · Bring the base-bid decision to the 6/17 review

Decision for John

The $2.86 base was held at John's direction in May. The new RightPricing pull shows eight sources fully locked out at the 301% ceiling (Section 04). Proposal: base $2.86 → $4.00 with all multipliers renormalized ×0.715 so current effective bids are preserved exactly — then re-target with full headroom. This is presented as Option B with complete math in the Right-Pricing report; no change is made without his sign-off.
